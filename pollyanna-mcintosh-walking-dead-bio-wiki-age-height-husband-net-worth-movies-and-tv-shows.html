Who is Pollyanna McIntosh?, Pollyanna McIntosh Biography and Wiki

Pollyanna McIntosh is a Scottish American writer, director, and actress who was born on March 15, 1979, in Scotland, United Kingdom. She is known for her roles in films The Woman (2011), Let Us Prey (2014), the first season of Joe R. Lansdale’s Hap along with Leonard (2016), a starring role in The Walking Dead (2017–2018), as well as social issue horror film Darlin’ (2019) which she wrote, directed and stars in. McIntosh also starred as Clara in the second season of Lodge 49.

McIntosh debuted as an actress aged 16 years, appearing in London’s independent film and theatre as both an actress and director. In 2004 McIntosh moved to Los Angeles and directed the stage production The Woolgatherer with Anne Dudek along with Dyan David Fisher.

In 2005, McIntosh received her first film role in Headspace. In 2009 McIntosh starred in Exam and was nominated for both Best Work of a Rookie at the BAFTA Awards and the Raindance Award at the British Independent Film Awards. In 2011, McIntosh was cast in the film The Woman for which she was nominated for Best Actress at the Fangoria Chainsaw Awards.

In 2016 McIntosh was cast as Angel in the SundanceTV series Hap and Leonard and was cast the following year as Jadis (“Anne”) in a starring role on The Walking Dead. McIntosh’s debut feature as a scriptwriter and director, the social issue horror film Darlin’, premiered at the South-by-Southwest festival in Austin, Texas in March the year 2019.

She also pursues a career in fashion and works in support of young people’s charity the Joshua Nolan Foundation. McIntosh was married to actor Grant Show from the year 2004 to 2011.
